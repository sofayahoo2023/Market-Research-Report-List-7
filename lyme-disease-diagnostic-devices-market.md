<p><p>The Lyme disease diagnostic devices market includes various testing types to identify the presence of the bacteria causing Lyme disease. Serological tests detect antibodies in the blood, while urine antigen tests identify specific Lyme antigens. The lymphocytic transformation test measures immune response, and immunofluorescent staining visualizes antibodies in tissues. Nucleic acid tests (NAT) detect the genetic material of the bacteria, providing high specificity and sensitivity. Each method has its advantages and limitations, catering to different diagnostic needs.</p></p>
