<p><p>The multi-function oscilloscope market is primarily divided into two types: digital oscilloscopes and analog oscilloscopes. Digital oscilloscopes capture and store waveforms as digital data, enabling advanced analysis, connectivity, and more precise measurements, making them suitable for complex applications. In contrast, analog oscilloscopes display waveforms directly on a screen using a continuous signal, offering real-time visualization. While digital oscilloscopes are prevalent in modern applications due to their versatility and features, analog oscilloscopes are valued for their simplicity and ease of use in basic tasks.</p></p>
