<p><ul><li>CCD</li><li>CMOS</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Interchangeable lens cameras primarily use two sensor types: CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) and CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor). CCD sensors offer high-quality images with excellent light sensitivity, making them ideal for low-light situations, but they tend to consume more power. In contrast, CMOS sensors are energy-efficient, enabling faster image processing and support for advanced features like video recording. The market for these cameras is driven by consumer demand for versatility, image quality, and technological advancements associated with each sensor type.</p></p>
