<p><p>The sagittal suture market comprises absorbable and non-absorbable sutures, each serving distinct purposes in surgical applications. Absorbable sutures are designed to gradually dissolve over time, eliminating the need for removal, making them ideal for internal tissue closure. Non-absorbable sutures, on the other hand, are durable and provide long-term support, often used in situations where strength is essential or for external applications. The choice between these types depends on the specific needs of the procedure and patient outcomes.</p></p>
