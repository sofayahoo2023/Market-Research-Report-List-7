<p><p>The VR tracker market is segmented into four types based on their tracking systems. </p><p>Optical systems utilize cameras and markers for precise tracking in 3D space. </p><p>Electromagnetic systems rely on magnetic fields and sensors for position tracking, offering robust performance in various environments. </p><p>Mechanical types use physical limbs or devices for movement detection, often seen in arcade settings. </p><p>Inertial systems incorporate accelerometers and gyroscopes to track motion, providing portability and ease of use in consumer-grade VR applications.</p></p>
