<p><p>The blood sugar monitoring equipment market comprises two main types: minimally invasive and non-invasive devices. Minimally invasive devices typically require a small puncture to obtain blood samples, allowing for accurate glucose readings through tools like lancets and continuous glucose monitors. Non-invasive devices, on the other hand, aim to measure blood sugar levels without breaking the skin, employing technologies such as infrared spectroscopy or electromagnetic waves. Both types cater to the growing demand for diabetes management solutions and patient-centric healthcare.</p></p>
